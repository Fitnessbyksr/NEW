Does this sound familiar?

Starting out — or getting back into it — can feel really daunting. If any of this resonates, you're not alone, and there's nothing wrong with you.

😬

You've joined a gym and barely gone — because it felt overwhelming

🚗

You've sat in the car park and driven home without going in

😕

You feel like everyone else got a manual you never received

💭

You've tried to "get fit first" before getting help — which never works

That's not a character flaw. It's a design problem.

I was a police officer for five years. When I left, my mental health was in a bad place and I'd stopped looking after myself completely. I joined a big commercial gym and quit after three weeks — sitting in the car park felt easier than going in.

That experience changed everything. It's why I became a PT, and exactly who I work with.
