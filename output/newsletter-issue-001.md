---

**Why I Almost Didn't Become a Personal Trainer**

I spent five years in the police. Two as a PCSO, three as an officer.

I don't regret it. I learned a lot, met good people, and did work that mattered. But by the time I left at the end of 2022, I was in a bad way.

The mental health had taken a knock. The weight had crept up. The sleep was shot. I'd been running on stress and bad habits for years and somewhere along the way I'd stopped looking after myself completely.

So I did what you're supposed to do. I joined a gym.

Got the trainers. Did the research. Paid the membership.

Three weeks later, I stopped going.

And I want to be honest about why — because I think a lot of people reading this will recognise it.

It wasn't laziness. I genuinely wanted to be there. But every single session I felt out of place. Everyone else seemed to know what they were doing. The equipment looked complicated. I didn't know the unwritten rules. I felt like I was the only one who hadn't been given the manual.

So the car park started winning. I'd drive there, sit for five minutes, and drive home again feeling worse than before I left.

I told myself I was just not a gym person.

Here's what I've figured out since then, after working with hundreds of clients who told me the exact same thing:

**The gym wasn't designed for people like me. And it isn't designed for most people.**

Big commercial gyms are built by people who love fitness for people who already love fitness. If you're new, anxious, returning after years away, or just not sure what you're doing — you're an afterthought. The environment assumes a level of confidence most people simply don't have yet.

That's not a character flaw. It's a design problem.

When I eventually found the right environment and the right support — everything changed. The weight came off. My mental health improved. I started looking forward to moving. And I thought: *I want to be that for someone else.*

That's why I became a PT. Not to shout at people or put them through boot camps. To be the person I needed when I was sitting in that car park.

That's what this newsletter is about. And that's who I work with.

So — glad you're here. Let's get started.

---

**This Week's Quick Win**

The smallest change that makes the biggest difference when you're getting started:

**Stop measuring success by how hard the session was. Measure it by whether you showed up.**

A ten-minute walk is infinitely better than a session you didn't do. The habit of showing up matters more than the intensity of what you do when you're there. Get the habit first. The rest follows.

**From the Gym Floor**

Had a new client come in for a consultation this week. She told me she'd driven past PT Factory three times before she actually came in.

Three times. Parked up, looked at the door, drove away.

She came in on the fourth time.

We had a 25-minute chat. Walked around the gym. I showed her where everything was. She left having booked her first session.

She said: *"I don't know what I was so scared of."*

That's the most common thing I hear after a free consultation. Which is exactly why I keep offering them.
