# WORKOUT 1
## Beginner Gym Workout — Full Body
### 3 days a week | ~45 minutes | Equipment: Gym machines

**Best for:** Complete beginners, people returning after a long break, anyone who wants a structured plan to start with.

**How to use it:** Do this workout 3 times a week with at least one rest day between sessions (e.g. Monday / Wednesday / Friday). Follow it for 4–6 weeks before moving on.

---

### Warm-Up — 10 minutes

Choose one cardio machine. Keep the pace comfortable — you should be able to hold a conversation.

- Treadmill walk (speed 5–6 km/h, incline 2)
- Stationary bike (easy resistance)
- Cross-trainer (easy pace)

---

### The Workout

**3 sets of 12 reps on each exercise.**
Rest 60–90 seconds between sets.
Choose a weight where the last 2–3 reps are an effort but your form stays solid.

---

**Exercise 1: Leg Press**
*Muscles: thighs, glutes*

- Sit in the machine, feet hip-width apart on the platform
- Push the platform away smoothly — stop before your knees fully lock
- Control it back slowly — don't let it drop
- Tip: if your lower back peels off the seat, the weight is too heavy or your feet are too low on the platform

---

**Exercise 2: Lat Pulldown**
*Muscles: upper back, biceps*

- Grip the bar wider than shoulder-width, palms facing away
- Pull the bar down to your upper chest, lean back slightly
- Squeeze at the bottom for a second
- Control the bar back up — don't just let it go
- Tip: think "elbows to your back pockets" to help engage the right muscles

---

**Exercise 3: Chest Press Machine**
*Muscles: chest, shoulders, triceps*

- Adjust the seat so the handles start at chest height
- Push forward until your arms are nearly straight
- Control back to the start — don't let the weight stack touch between reps
- Tip: keep your shoulder blades pressed back into the pad throughout

---

**Exercise 4: Seated Cable Row**
*Muscles: mid-back, biceps*

- Sit tall with a slight bend in your knees
- Pull the handle into your lower chest or upper stomach
- Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end
- Slowly return to the start — let your back stretch forward slightly
- Tip: don't hunch forward to reach the handle — use a lighter weight if needed

---

**Exercise 5: Leg Extension**
*Muscles: quads (front of thighs)*

- Sit in the machine, pad resting just above your ankles
- Straighten your legs fully
- Hold for a second at the top
- Lower slowly — 2–3 seconds on the way down
- Tip: point your toes slightly upward to feel more in the quads

---

**Exercise 6: Shoulder Press Machine**
*Muscles: shoulders, upper arms*

- Adjust seat so handles begin at shoulder height
- Press up until your arms are nearly straight — don't lock out hard
- Control the weight back down
- Tip: don't let your shoulders shrug up toward your ears

---

### Cool-Down — 5–10 minutes

Hold each stretch for 20–30 seconds. Breathe through it — don't bounce.

- **Quad stretch:** Stand, pull one foot up behind you toward your backside. Hold a wall if needed.
- **Hamstring stretch:** Sit on floor, legs straight. Reach toward your toes. Feel it in the back of your thighs.
- **Chest stretch:** Stand tall, arms wide, gently squeeze shoulder blades together.
- **Lat stretch:** Reach one arm above your head, lean to the opposite side.
- **Neck rolls:** Slow, gentle rolls side to side. Not full circles.

---

### Progression

Every 2 weeks, increase the weight by one notch on each exercise.
If a weight stops you completing 12 reps with good form, drop back and build up again.
If 12 reps feels too easy before 2 weeks, go up.

# WORKOUT 2
## Home Workout — No Equipment
### 3 days a week | ~30 minutes | No equipment needed

**Best for:** Anyone who isn't ready for the gym yet, people who want to exercise at home, those building a baseline fitness level before joining a gym.

**How to use it:** Do 3 days a week, rest days between. Even two days a week is a start — don't let "I can't do all three" become an excuse to do none.

---

### Warm-Up — 5 minutes

Do each movement for 30 seconds, repeat twice.

- March on the spot (lift your knees)
- Arm circles forward × 10, then backward × 10
- Hip circles (hands on hips, big circles each way)
- Slow bodyweight squats × 5 (just moving, not working yet)
- Shoulder rolls, forward and back

---

### The Workout

**3 rounds of the following circuit.**
Take a 60-second rest between rounds.
Go at a pace that challenges you without wiping you out.

---

**1. Bodyweight Squat — 12 reps**
*Muscles: thighs, glutes*

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower yourself like you're sitting back into a chair. Go as low as feels comfortable — aim for thighs parallel to the floor if you can. Push through your heels to stand back up. Keep your chest up.

*Easier: shallower squat, hold a chair for balance*
*Harder: pause at the bottom for 2 seconds*

---

**2. Press-Up — 8–12 reps**
*Muscles: chest, shoulders, triceps*

Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, body in a straight line. Lower your chest to just above the floor. Push back up.

*Easier: knees on the floor (this is a legitimate, effective exercise — not a "cheat")*
*Harder: feet elevated on a chair or sofa*

---

**3. Glute Bridge — 12 reps**
*Muscles: glutes, lower back, hamstrings*

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Push your hips up until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze at the top. Lower slowly.

*Harder: hold at the top for 3 seconds each rep*

---

**4. Standing Row with Resistance Band — 12 reps**
*(Or: skip this one if you don't have a band)*
*Muscles: back, biceps*

Attach a resistance band to a door handle or fixed point at waist height. Hold both ends, step back until there's tension. Pull your elbows back and hands to your sides. Squeeze your shoulder blades. Slowly return.

*No band? Do a doorframe row instead: stand in a doorway, hold each side, lean back and pull yourself toward it.*

---

**5. Reverse Lunge — 10 reps each leg**
*Muscles: thighs, glutes, balance*

Stand tall. Step one foot back and lower your back knee toward the floor. Front knee stays above your ankle. Push through the front foot to return to standing. Alternate legs.

*Easier: hold a wall or chair for balance*
*Harder: add a small pause at the bottom*

---

**6. Superman Hold — 10 reps**
*Muscles: lower back, glutes*

Lie face down, arms extended in front of you. Lift your arms and legs off the floor simultaneously. Hold for 2 seconds. Lower slowly. This one's great for your lower back — an area most people neglect.

---

**7. Dead Bug — 10 reps**
*Muscles: core*

Lie on your back, arms pointing to the ceiling, knees bent at 90 degrees (like you're sitting in an invisible chair). Slowly lower your right arm behind your head and your left leg to hover just above the floor. Return to start. Repeat on the other side. Keep your lower back pressed into the floor throughout.

---

### Cool-Down — 5 minutes

- Child's pose — 30 seconds
- Hip flexor stretch — kneel on one knee, push hips forward, 30 seconds each side
- Lying twist — on your back, drop both knees to one side, arms wide. 30 seconds each side.
- Deep breathing — 5 slow breaths. You're done.

---

### Progression

Week 1–2: 2 rounds
Week 3–4: 3 rounds
Week 5+: 3 rounds, start adding reps or slowing the movements down

# WORKOUT 3
## Mobility & Movement Routine
### Daily or 3–5 times a week | ~20 minutes | No equipment

**Best for:** Older adults, anyone with stiff joints, people who've been told to "move more" but aren't sure how, anyone who spends a lot of time sitting.

**Also good for:** As a warm-up before any other workout, or as a standalone routine on rest days.

**How to use it:** Do it in the morning if possible — it'll make a real difference to how your body feels through the day. Can also be done in the evening to wind down.

---

### A Note on This Routine

Mobility work is one of the most underrated parts of fitness. It won't make you look different in the mirror — but it'll change how you feel. Moving without pain, getting up from the floor without a struggle, turning your head without stiffness — these things matter, especially as we get older.

This routine takes 20 minutes. It's gentle. It's manageable. And if you do it consistently, you'll notice the difference within two or three weeks.

---

### The Routine

Hold each position for the time listed. Breathe slowly and steadily throughout — don't hold your breath.

---

**1. Neck Mobility — 2 minutes**

Sit or stand tall. Move slowly through each:
- Chin to chest and back × 5
- Ear to shoulder (left and right) × 5 each
- Slow look left, then right × 5 each
- Half circles (chin to chest, roll to one ear, back to centre, roll to the other ear) × 5

*Move within a comfortable range. Stop if anything hurts.*

---

**2. Shoulder Circles — 1 minute**

Arms hanging by your sides. Roll both shoulders:
- Forward × 10
- Backward × 10

Then: reach one arm across your body and hold with the other arm. 30 seconds each side.

---

**3. Thoracic Rotation (Upper Back) — 2 minutes**

Sit in a chair with your feet flat on the floor. Cross your arms over your chest. Slowly rotate your upper body to the left as far as is comfortable. Hold for 2 seconds. Return to centre. Rotate right. Repeat × 10 each side.

This one is excellent for anyone who sits at a desk.

---

**4. Hip Flexor Stretch — 2 minutes**

Kneel on one knee on a soft surface (mat or carpet), other foot flat on the floor in front.

Gently push your hips forward until you feel a stretch at the front of the hip of the kneeling leg. Hold. Don't lean forward — the movement comes from the hips.

Hold 45 seconds each side. Repeat if time allows.

*Option if kneeling is uncomfortable: stand and do a slow lunge instead, same stretch position.*

---

**5. Hip Circles — 1 minute**

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hands on hips. Make big slow circles with your hips — like a hula hoop.

10 circles clockwise. 10 circles counter-clockwise.

---

**6. Ankle Circles — 1 minute**

Sit in a chair. Lift one foot slightly and trace slow circles with your foot — big circles.

10 clockwise, 10 counter-clockwise. Swap feet.

Great for anyone whose ankles are stiff in the morning.

---

**7. Knee-to-Chest Pull — 2 minutes**

Lie on your back. Pull one knee gently toward your chest. Hold for 30 seconds. Swap sides. Repeat.

Then: pull both knees to your chest at the same time, rock gently side to side. 30 seconds.

---

**8. Seated Forward Fold — 2 minutes**

Sit on the floor with legs straight (or slightly bent if needed). Reach forward toward your feet. Go as far as is comfortable — you don't need to touch your toes.

Hold for 60 seconds. Breathe into the stretch.

*Chair version: sit on the edge of a chair, legs out, reach toward your feet.*

---

**9. Cat-Cow — 2 minutes**

On hands and knees (or seated on the edge of a chair if the floor isn't an option).

**Cat:** Round your back up toward the ceiling, tuck your chin to your chest.
**Cow:** Arch your back downward, lift your head and chest.

Move slowly between the two. 10 repetitions.

This is one of the best exercises in existence for back stiffness.

---

**10. Child's Pose — 2 minutes**

From hands and knees, sit your hips back toward your heels. Reach your arms forward on the floor. Let your forehead rest down.

Hold and breathe. This is the cool-down.

*If this position isn't comfortable, lie on your back with your knees pulled gently to your chest instead.*

---

### How Long Until You Notice a Difference?

Most people notice something within 2–3 weeks of doing this consistently. Easier to get out of bed. Less stiffness in the morning. Fewer aches through the day.

Stick at it. Mobility is one of those things that pays off quietly — and you notice most when you stop doing it.
