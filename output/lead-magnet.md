### Email 2 — Day 1
**Subject:** The real reason you keep stopping (it's not what you think)
**Preview:** This one took me years to figure out...

Hi [First name],

Can I tell you something a bit personal?

Three years ago I was a police officer. I was putting on weight, sleeping badly, and honestly — I wasn't in a good place mentally.

I tried the gym. Hated it. Felt out of place, didn't know what I was doing, and quit within weeks.

Sound familiar?

Here's what I figured out eventually: it wasn't me. It was the environment.

Big gyms are designed for people who already know what they're doing. If you're new, anxious, or just not sure where to start — they're actually the worst place to begin.

I left the police at the end of 2022. Fitness became the thing that genuinely turned my life around. And once I had the right environment and someone in my corner — everything changed.

That's why I became a PT.

Not to shout at people or put them through boot camps. But to be the person I wish I'd had when I was standing in that car park, dreading going in.

Tomorrow I'll tell you what actually makes the difference between people who stick and people who don't.

Cheers,
Kallum

### Email 3 — Day 3
**Subject:** What I'd do if I was starting from scratch tomorrow
**Preview:** Honestly? I wouldn't go near a gym for at least a week.

Hi [First name],

Right — practical stuff today.

If I was starting completely from scratch, feeling nervous, a bit out of shape, not sure where to begin — here's genuinely what I'd do.

**1. I'd stop trying to overhaul everything at once.**
The people who try to change their diet, start going to the gym 5 days a week, and cut out everything they enjoy — they last about 10 days. Small, consistent changes beat dramatic ones every time.

**2. I'd find one person who's been where I am.**
Not a PT with a six-pack who's never struggled. Someone who gets it. Someone I can actually talk to.

**3. I'd go and see what a gym actually looks like before I committed.**
Half the anxiety is not knowing what to expect. Walk in, have a look around, ask questions. No commitment.

That last one is literally what my free consultation is — a tour of PT Factory in Denton and a chat with me about what you want to achieve. No exercise. No hard sell. Just a conversation.

But more on that later in the week.

Tomorrow I want to tell you about one of my clients. He couldn't get himself up off the floor when we started. What happened next is something I'm genuinely proud of.

Speak tomorrow,
Kallum
