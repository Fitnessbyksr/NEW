## Handle Options

Five options across a spectrum — from personal brand to angle-led. Same handle on both platforms is ideal for consistency.

---

### Option 1: `@kallumrolfe_pt` *(Recommended)*
**Why:** Personal brands outperform business names on social in 2024/25. People follow people. "Kallum Rolfe" is searchable, memorable, and grows with you beyond just personal training. The `_pt` keeps it findable.

---

### Option 2: `@kallum.pt`
**Why:** Clean, short, professional. Easy to say out loud ("follow me at kallum dot pt"). Works brilliantly on TikTok where brevity wins. Less likely to be taken than the full name.

---

### Option 3: `@fitnessbyksr`
**Why:** Already consistent with your website (fitnessbyksr.co.uk) and any existing audience. If you've got a following, the safest option — no loss of recognition.

---

### Option 4: `@ksrfitness`
**Why:** A small tidy-up from the current. Shorter than fitnessbyksr, slightly cleaner. Still consistent with the brand initials. Easy to remember.

---

### Option 5: `@hatethe_gym`
**Why:** Bold. Memorable. Captures the exact angle. Someone anxious about the gym searches "hate the gym" and you literally come up. High risk, high reward — this is a content-creator handle more than a PT handle.

---

## My Pick for You

**`@kallumrolfe_pt`** on both platforms.

Here's why: your story is the brand. Kallum who left the police, struggled with his mental health, and found his way back through fitness — that's what makes you different from every other PT in Manchester. A personal name handle tells people they're following a real person. It also gives you more flexibility if you ever branch beyond PT (online courses, speaking, content).
