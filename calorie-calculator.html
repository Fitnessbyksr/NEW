How to use your results

A few things worth knowing before you dive in.

Maintenance calories is the number of calories your body needs each day to stay exactly as you are — not losing weight, not gaining it. Everything above that creates a surplus (useful for building muscle), everything below creates a deficit (useful for losing fat).

Protein keeps you full, preserves muscle when you're losing weight, and provides the building blocks your body needs to recover from exercise. Most people eating a typical diet get about half as much as they need. The goal shown is based on your bodyweight — aim to get close to it across the day rather than all at once.

The calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which research shows is the most accurate formula for most people. It'll get you within 10–15% of your actual needs. Use it as a starting point, track your weight for 2–3 weeks, and adjust up or down depending on what's happening. Real-world feedback beats any formula.
