## Voice in 3 Words

**Genuine. Warm. Buzzing.**

## The Voice Persona

Fitnessbyksr sounds like a big lad from Manchester who just genuinely loves what he does and wants everyone around him to feel good about themselves. He never talks down to anyone, never reaches for a fitness term when a plain word will do, and always remembers that you've got a life outside the gym. When he writes, it feels like a text from a mate who happens to know exactly how to help you. He's the kind of person who asks about your kids, remembers you've been struggling with your knee, and makes you feel like his favourite client — because in that moment, you are. He's imposing when he needs to be. Mostly he's a big soft teddy bear who's just buzzing to see you.

## Core Beliefs (Brand Worldview)

1. The gym should feel welcoming, full stop — intimidation is the enemy of progress
2. Everyone deserves to feel strong — regardless of age, size, gender, race, or sexuality
3. Fitness only sticks if you actually enjoy it — results come from fun sessions, not punishment
4. The mental side matters as much as the physical — Kallum's been there himself
5. Nobody is too far gone, too old, or too unfit — you just need the right person in your corner
6. You don't have to change your whole life — just show up consistently and let the rest follow
7. Being strong doesn't mean being hard — you can be powerful and gentle at the same time

## Vocabulary Guide

### Words and phrases we use
- **Mate** — essential, used in almost every direct communication
- **Buzzing** — genuine enthusiasm only, don't force it
- **Cheers / cheers mate** — natural sign-offs
- **Brilliant / class / top** — Northern approval words
- **Get you in / get started / get going** — action-forward, low pressure
- **Genuinely / honestly** — authenticity markers, use sparingly so they land
- **Give it a go / give it a crack** — low-pressure invitation language
- **Real results / actually works / proper** — plain emphasis, no hype
- **Feel like yourself again** — the emotional core of the brand
- **No judgement / no pressure** — welcoming language, used especially with anxious first-timers
- **I've been there** — origin story callback

### Words we never use
- Shred / shredded
- Grind / hustle / beast mode
- Transformation (say "change" or "results" instead — less intimidating)
- Gains (too gym-bro)
- Macros / TDEE / progressive overload (in public-facing copy — fine in client programming)
- Elite / premium / exclusive (sounds cold and corporate)
- Journey (overused to meaninglessness)
- Empower / empowering (corporate wellness speak)
- Crush it / kill it / destroy (aggressive gym language)
- Bespoke (too fancy for who we are)

### Signature expressions
- "Buzzing to [do/share/work with] you"
- "Cheers mate"
- "Give it a go"
- "No pressure, no judgement"
- "Feel like yourself again"
- "I've been there"
- "Whoever you are, whatever your starting point"

## Sentence & Paragraph Rules

- **Sentences:** Short and punchy when making a point. Allowed to run longer when telling a story or being warm. Never academic.
- **Paragraphs:** 1–3 lines maximum in emails and social. White space is essential.
- **Headlines:** Sentence case always — not Title Case (too formal). Questions and direct statements over clever wordplay.
- **Punctuation:** Em dashes for a beat — like this. Ellipsis for trailing warmth... Exclamation marks maximum one per message — overuse kills the energy.
- **Contractions:** Always. "You're" not "you are." "It's" not "it is." No exceptions.
- **Person:** Always "I" — never "we." This is Kallum, one real person.
- **Capitalisation:** Casual in direct comms (social DMs, emails) mirrors how Kallum actually writes. Standard caps in formal copy (website, ads).

---

## What We Are NOT

1. **We are NOT a military fitness brand** — we never use "no excuses," "earn it," or language that treats exercise as punishment. Clients come to feel better, not to be flogged.
2. **We are NOT a corporate wellness service** — we never sound like a brochure. No "bespoke packages," no "holistic wellness journey." Plain words. Real talk.
3. **We are NOT the loud, aggressive gym-bro PT** — we never flex our own physique to sell sessions, never make clients feel small about their starting point, and never make it about us.

## Voice Checklist (use before publishing anything)

- [ ] Does this sound like a text from Kallum, not a PT website?
- [ ] Would someone who hates the gym feel welcomed, not intimidated?
- [ ] Is there at least one specific, real detail — not generic fitness claims?
- [ ] Does it avoid the forbidden words list?
- [ ] Is the tone right for this context?
- [ ] Plain English — would a 14-year-old understand it?
